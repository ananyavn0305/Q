🔹 Unit I: Embedded Systems
Q1. What is an Embedded System?
A system designed to perform a specific function within a larger system.
It combines hardware and software for dedicated tasks.
Q2. What are characteristics of Embedded Systems?
They are real-time, reliable, and resource-constrained systems.
They are designed for specific applications with minimal user interaction.
Q3. What is a Real-Time System?
A system that responds within a strict time constraint.
Output correctness depends on both result and timing.
Q4. What are Real-Time Tasks?
Tasks that must be executed within deadlines.
They are periodic or event-driven.
Q5. Difference between General Processor and Embedded Processor?
General processors handle multiple tasks, embedded processors are task-specific.
Embedded processors are optimized for efficiency and low power.
Q6. What is a Microcontroller?
A compact integrated circuit with CPU, memory, and I/O.
Used for controlling embedded applications.
Q7. Properties of Microcontrollers?
Low power consumption and compact size.
Integrated peripherals like timers and ADC.
Q8. Components of Microcontroller?
CPU, RAM, ROM, I/O ports, timers.
Also includes ADC(Analog-to-Digital Converter), DAC(Digital-to-Analog Converter), and communication interfaces.
Q9. What is System-on-Chip (SoC)?
An integrated chip with all system components.
Used in smartphones and IoT devices.
Q10. Examples of SoC?
Raspberry Pi SoC and mobile processors.
Used in smart devices.
Q11. Components of Embedded Systems?
Hardware, software, and real-time OS.
Includes sensors and actuators.
Q12. What is Embedded Processor?
Processor designed for embedded applications.
Optimized for specific control tasks.
🔹 Unit II: IoT Concepts
Q13. What is IoT?
A network of interconnected physical devices.
Devices collect and exchange data via internet.
Q14. Characteristics of IoT?
Connectivity, intelligence, scalability.
Self-configuration and dynamic nature.
Q15. Trends in IoT Adoption?
Rapid growth in smart devices and automation.
Used in healthcare, agriculture, smart homes.
Q16. What are IoT Devices?
Devices connected to internet with sensors/actuators.
Example: smart watches, smart bulbs.
Q17. IoT Devices vs Computers?
IoT devices are task-specific and low-power.
Computers are general-purpose systems.
Q18. Basic Building Blocks of IoT?
Sensors, connectivity, processing, user interface.
Cloud and analytics also included.
Q19. What are “Things” in IoT?
Physical objects connected to internet.
They collect and share data.
Q20. What is Interoperability in IoT?
Ability of devices to communicate across platforms.
Ensures seamless integration.
Q21. What are Sensors?
Devices that detect physical changes.
Convert them into electrical signals.
Q22. What are Actuators?
Devices that perform actions based on signals.
Example: motors, LEDs.
Q23. Why Analog/Digital Conversion is needed?
Sensors produce analog signals.
Microcontrollers process digital signals.
Q24. IoT Functional Blocks?
Devices, communication, services, management.
Also includes security and applications.
Q25. IoT Enabling Technologies?
Cloud computing, big data, AI.
Wireless communication technologies.
Q26. IoT Levels and Deployment Templates?
Defines system complexity and architecture.
Ranges from simple to complex deployments.
Q27. Applications of IoT?
Smart homes, healthcare, agriculture.
Industrial automation and smart cities.
